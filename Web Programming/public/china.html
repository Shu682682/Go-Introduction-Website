Go Information Introduction Website

Go is a strategic chess game that uses a grid-shaped chessboard and black and white chess pieces. It originated in China. In ancient China, it had many names such as "chess", "qi", and "hand talk". It belongs to the four arts of chess, calligraphy and painting.

China Go

On March 15, 1992, the founding ceremony of the China Chess Academy was held grandly. The Chinese Chess Academy broke ground in October 1988 and took more than three years to finally complete. The Chinese Chess Academy is a public institution directly under the National Sports Commission. It is a comprehensive training base and competition venue for Chinese chess sports. It directly manages the national team training tasks for the national Go team, chess and international chess, and undertakes various domestic and international competitions for the three chess games. Major competitions, and various mass chess and card activities are also carried out.

In November 2019, the Chess and Card Sports Management Center of the State Sports General Administration (China Chess Academy) was awarded the protection unit of the national intangible cultural heritage representative project "Xiangqi".

China Go League A

In the first year, only ten teams participated, with two rounds of home and away games and 18 games. In each subsequent competition, there were twelve teams, with two rounds of home and away games and 22 games. Each club can send 4 of the 6 players to participate. The team members are not limited to players from the Chinese Chess Academy. A large number of Korean and Japanese foreign players appear in the A and even B leagues.

The last two teams in League A are relegated to China Go League B, and the top two teams in League B are promoted to League A. Since 2006, there has been a championship match between the league champion and the South Korean league champion.
