What is Go

According to legend, Go was invented by Emperor Yao around 2300 BC. His son Danzhu was proud, arrogant, and willful, so Emperor Yao invented Go to cultivate his son's character.

Go has two kinds of chess pieces, black and white. It is stipulated that the player holding the black chess piece goes first. Both sides of the game alternately place black and white chess pieces at the intersection points on the 19x19 line chessboard grid. After the move is completed, you cannot regret the move. During the game of chess, the size of the surrounding "land" determines the outcome.

The rules of Go are simple and elegant, but the gameplay is ever-changing. Mastering its connotations requires a lot of practice and study. Chess master Emmanuel Lasker praised: "If there are other intelligent life forms in the universe, they will almost certainly know Go." New Go Introduction

After the Meiji Restoration, shogi players lost the support of the shogunate and began to seek new means of livelihood, which led to the emergence of news chess games and the modern ranking system, and the establishment of a nationwide Japanese chess academy. During the Showa era, Wu Qingyuan and Kitani Minoru jointly set off the trend of "new layout" and began the era of modern Go. Later, many first-class chess players emerged in the Japanese chess world, such as Eio Sakata, Hideyuki Fujisawa, Katsura Takagawa, and later Hideo Otake, Yoshio Ishida, Masaki Takemiya, Koichi Kobayashi, etc.

In 1989, South Korean Cho Hoon-hyun won the first Yingshi Cup World Go Championship, which also triggered a boom in Go in South Korea. Since then, a large number of world chess games have appeared. In these chess battles, South Korea's Lee Chang-ho stood out from many chess players and became the number one player in the chess world at that time. After 2006, Lee Chang-ho's status declined, and emerging forces such as Lee Sedol and Gu Li began to attack him. The next few years were the era of the two heroes Gu Li and Li Shidou competing for hegemony.

In the 2010s, many Chinese chess players emerged. Young chess players such as Jiang Weijie, Fan Tingyu, Shi Yue, Mi Yuting, Chen Yaoye, Zhou Ruiyang, and Tan Xiao successively won world championships.

Go Culture

During the thousands of years of spread of Go, many terms have gradually emerged. Some of them have entered the public vocabulary, such as "ending" and so on. Almost every move in Go has related terminology, and it is even possible to completely describe a local battle in words. For example: Black occupies the star position in the upper right corner, White Xiao flies up, Black doubles with a high trap, White jumps with one, Black follows, White flies into the corner, Black three and three block... This passage uses Go terminology to vividly describe common setting stones. journey. Common terms include:

  • Called capture: When a chess piece has only one energy, it is called capture. Also known as "calling to eat".
  • Qi: At the empty intersection that is closely adjacent to the chess piece on the chessboard, the qi number of a chess piece cannot exceed four qi, but the connected chess pieces may have more than four qi. Tight placement of pieces on the opponent's way to life is called "tight air".
  • Lift: The empty chess pieces should be lifted and taken away from the chessboard. Lifting and capturing the opponent's chess pieces is called "lifting pieces".
  • Eye: On the chessboard, the blank intersection surrounded by a piece of chess is called the "eye".
  • Land: The sum of the number of objects surrounded by live chess pieces and the live chess pieces themselves is called "land".
  • Empty: an area surrounded by chess pieces.
  • Tribulation: A situation in which both sides can take turns extracting each other's pieces. The rules of Go stipulate that when robbing, the party who was extracted cannot directly withdraw it, but must find the robbery material elsewhere to make the other party respond before withdrawing it.
